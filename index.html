LET'S TALK ABOUT MENSTURAL HEALTH!

About us

Menstruation, or getting period, is a normal and healthy part of life for most women. However, it’s still stigmatized in many places. Some girls and women feel embarrassed or ashamed when they have to address their periods. It can even cause some people to avoid certain activities and miss out on important experiences. That’s why menstruation awareness and events like National Period Day on 10 October and Menstrual hygiene Day on 28 May are so important.



Features

01.
Period tracker
Tracking your menstrual cycle simply means keeping a record of when you’re menstruating and documenting your cycle. It is beneficial for women who are trying to conceive. It also help in unwanted pregnancies and the more intimately you know your own body and its cycles, the more easily you can recognize when something is amiss.

Blogs and Awareness
Read about some women's first period stories to figure out if everyone goes through the same thing. Turns out, there are some instances that have happened with quite a few, while there are others that are a little on the once in a blue moon spectrum. Who doesn’t like to hear about other people’s period stories?
03.
Our Products
Many women cannot afford menstrual health products to meet their monthly needs, and this may impact their mental well-being. Improved access to affordable menstrual products is needed to support these women. Feminine hygiene products are necessities, not luxuries, and should be treated as such.
